Go to **Profile → Security** tab to manage your account safety.

## Changing your password

1. Go to the **Security** tab.
2. Click **Change Password**.
3. Enter your **current password**.
4. Enter and confirm your **new password**.
5. Click **Save**.

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security. After enabling it, you'll need both your password and a code from an authenticator app on your phone to sign in.

### Setting up 2FA

1. Go to the **Security** tab.
2. Click **Enable Two-Factor Authentication**.
3. A QR code appears. Scan it with an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, Microsoft Authenticator, etc.).
4. Enter the 6-digit code from the app to confirm.
5. **Save your recovery codes.** These are 8 one-time codes you can use if you lose access to your authenticator app. Write them down or store them somewhere safe — not on your phone.

<Aside type="caution">
If you lose both your authenticator app and your recovery codes, you will be locked out of your account. You'll need to contact your school's IT office for help.
</Aside>

### Managing 2FA

Once 2FA is set up, you can:

- **Disable 2FA**: Click **Disable** and enter your password to confirm.
- **Regenerate recovery codes**: Click to get a new set of 8 codes. Old codes become invalid.
- **Login challenges**: Toggle on to require 2FA every time you sign in. Toggle off to only require it periodically.
- **Email authentication**: Toggle on to also receive login codes by email as an alternative to your authenticator app.

## Managing browser sessions

The **Browser Sessions** section shows all devices where your account is currently signed in.

- Each session shows the device, browser, IP address, and last active time.
- Your current session is marked with "This device."
- If you see a session you don't recognize, or used a shared computer:

1. Click **Log Out Other Browser Sessions**.
2. Enter your password to confirm.
3. All other sessions are signed out immediately.

## Passkeys (passwordless login)

Passkeys let you sign in without typing a password — using your fingerprint, face, or device PIN instead.

### Setting up a passkey

1. Go to **Security → Passkeys**.
2. Click **Add Passkey**.
3. Your browser will prompt you to verify your identity (Touch ID, Windows Hello, etc.).
4. Give the passkey a name (e.g. "My MacBook" or "iPhone").
5. The passkey is saved.

### Using a passkey to sign in

Next time you sign in, choose **Sign in with Passkey** instead of entering your password. Your device will prompt you for your fingerprint or face.

### Removing a passkey

If you lose a device or no longer want to use a passkey, click the **delete icon** next to it in the Passkeys list.
